2. Modeling rules and equations

  • A_footprint = L x B
  • A_effective,deck = A_footprint x deck_factor
  • A_net,deck = A_effective,deck x net_gross_factor
  • W_deck = A_effective x w_structure + A_net x (w_outfit + w_payload)
  • Delta = rho x L x B x T x Cb, with seawater density 1.025 t/m3.
  • KG_total = sum(W_i x KG_i) / sum(W_i). This report includes deck-level KG bands for the next-stage moment model.

All densities are central planning values. Use the CSVs to run low/base/high sweeps before committing to any hydrostatic or structural basis.

7. Red flags that can break the model

  1. Upper-deck mass: parks, pools, soil, stadiums, and heavy civic spaces high above baseline can consume stability margin even if displacement is available.
  2. Free surface: ballast, pools, water features, wastewater tanks, and large liquid stores must be compartmented and included in GM correction.
  3. Evacuation/refuge: 100,000-person surge requires district refuge, smoke control, protected stairs, and external evacuation capacity; area alone does not prove safety.
  4. Utility independence: power, desalination, wastewater, district cooling, fuel, exhaust, and fire segregation may require more technical volume than area heuristics assume.
  5. Structural unknown: global bending/torsion across 1.6 km and modular joint fatigue may drive structure far above simple t/m2 assumptions.
  6. Vehicle policy: private cars are a design fork. They add mass, fire risk, ventilation, parking area, ramps, and structural loads.
  7. Regulatory basis: class/flag/IMO equivalency can force compartment, refuge, egress, lifesaving, and machinery redundancy penalties not captured here.
