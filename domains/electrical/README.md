# Power, Electrical & Controls — community charter

An Open Deck community of practice for **electrical, power-systems, and
instrumentation & controls** engineering on offshore and marine assets. Public,
open, non-proprietary. Discussion-first today; automated analysis comes later.

## Who it's for
Electrical & power-systems engineers, instrumentation & controls (I&C)
engineers, subsea-power / umbilical specialists, and electrification /
power-distribution engineers working offshore and marine.

## What it covers
- Power systems — generation, distribution, load flow, fault levels, protection
  coordination, harmonics, grounding.
- Instrumentation & controls — sensing, control philosophy, SIS/SIL concepts,
  signal architecture.
- Subsea power & umbilicals — power delivery to subsea loads, electrical
  umbilical sizing concepts.
- Electrification — platform/vessel electrification, shore power, drivetrain
  electrification trade-offs.
- Codes & standards — IEC, IEEE, API RP 14F/14FZ, DNV electrical rules and the
  methods that flow from them.

## How it answers (read before posting)
- **Discussion-first / preliminary engineering.** Methods, codes, standards,
  scoping and order-of-magnitude sizing — *not* build-ready design and *not*
  full automated calculations yet. Say so plainly when an ask needs proper
  analysis, and outline what that would take.
- **Assumptions before numbers.** Every quantitative reply states its
  assumptions (3–5 lines) up front; headline numbers are sanity-checked ranges,
  not point claims. Anything substantial ends with an Assumptions & Sensitivity
  note.
- **Open material only.** No client data, no licensed or proprietary content,
  no NDA-bound drawings. When in doubt, keep it general.
- **In domain.** Off-domain asks get a friendly one-line redirect back to
  electrical/power/I&C work. IT/computer questions are not this channel.

## Channel identity (capability-led, ~400 chars)

mature it absorbs `instrumentation`, `controls`, `subsea-power`, and
`electrification`, added per the community→route binding mechanics in #140.

is operative — keep it accurate; tightening it narrows what the community can
reach at runtime.
