# Manufacturing & Fabrication — community charter

An Open Deck community of practice for **fabrication, welding, machining and
production** of offshore and marine hardware. Public, open, non-proprietary.
Discussion-first today; this community owns applied shop-floor and yard
practice, not code interpretation.

## Who it's for
Fabrication and welding engineers, NDE/inspection personnel, machinists and
manufacturing engineers, production planners, and QA/QC engineers working on
offshore and marine equipment and structures.

## What it covers
- Fabrication — fit-up, fixturing, distortion control, dimensional control,
  yard and shop fabrication sequences.
- Welding & NDE — process selection, weld procedure and welder qualification
  practice (WPS/PQR/WQT at a working level), inspection methods (VT/RT/UT/MT/PT)
  and when to use which.
- Machining — machining processes, tolerances and surface finish in practice,
  machinability trade-offs.
- Production planning — work packaging, sequencing, capacity and throughput
  concepts for fabrication scopes.
- Manufacturing QA/QC — inspection & test plans (ITPs), hold/witness points,
  traceability, nonconformance handling, as-built records.

## What goes to Codes, Standards & Maritime Law instead
Pure code-interpretation questions — *what does the standard say*, which
edition governs, what a clause intends (e.g. AWS D1.1, ASME IX, ISO 3834, DNV
fabrication rules) — belong in the **Codes, Standards & Maritime Law**
community, the cross-cutting advisory layer. This community owns the applied
side: *how do I fabricate, weld, inspect or qualify* to meet that requirement.
When a thread turns into clause exegesis, give a friendly one-line redirect
there; when a codes thread turns into shop practice, it lands here.

## How it answers (read before posting)
- **Discussion-first / applied practice.** Methods, process selection, QA/QC
  practice and producibility feedback — *not* certified weld procedures, *not*
  shop-ready travelers, and *not* full automated calculations yet. Say so
  plainly when an ask needs qualified engineering or a certified body, and
  outline what that would take.
- **Assumptions before numbers.** Every quantitative reply states its
  assumptions (3–5 lines) up front; headline numbers are sanity-checked ranges,
  not point claims. Anything substantial ends with an Assumptions & Sensitivity
  note.
- **Open material only.** No client data, no licensed or proprietary content,
  no NDA-bound drawings or procedures. When in doubt, keep it general.
- **In domain.** Off-domain asks get a friendly one-line redirect back to
  fabrication/manufacturing work. IT/computer questions are not this channel.

## Growth
Starts as a single discussion-first community anchored on the `manufacturing`
route. As routes mature it can absorb welding, NDE and production-planning
sub-routes, added per the community→route binding mechanics in #140.

## Governance

is operative — keep it accurate; tightening it narrows what the community can
reach at runtime.
