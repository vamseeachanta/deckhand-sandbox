# Subsea, Pipelines & Integrity — community charter

An Open Deck community of practice for **fixed and subsea structures plus the
integrity & code-check workflow** on offshore assets. Public, open,
non-proprietary. Pipeline, structural and geotechnical topics can run real
analysis; integrity and CFD are advisory-first for now.

## Who it's for
Pipeline engineers, subsea structural/fatigue engineers, geotechnical engineers,
integrity / fitness-for-service engineers, and CFD analysts.

## What it covers
- Pipelines — wall thickness, freespan, on-bottom stability, capacity, pipelay.
- Structural — fatigue (S-N, rainflow), pipe/plate capacity, jackets, FEA
  assessment.
- Geotechnical — soil bearing, mudmat stability, foundation design.
- Asset integrity — fitness-for-service, corrosion, cathodic protection, life
  extension.
- CFD — flow-induced loads where they drive the structural check.

## How it answers (read before posting)
- **Runnable where we can, advisory elsewhere.** Pipeline, structural and
  geotechnical have worked examples we can run; asset-integrity and CFD are
  discussion/advisory-first until they can compute. Say so plainly when an ask
  needs proper analysis, and outline what that would take.
- **Codes up front.** This area lives on DNV/API — name the governing code and
  edition behind any check.
- **Assumptions before numbers.** Every quantitative reply states its
  assumptions (3–5 lines) up front; headline numbers are sanity-checked ranges,
  not point claims. Anything substantial ends with an Assumptions & Sensitivity
  note.
- **Cite licensed sources, never reproduce them.** No client data and no
  NDA-bound drawings — ever. The assistant may draw on engineering standards the
  operator is licensed for (DNV, API, ASME, …) and must **cite them by code and
  clause** (e.g. "DNV-OS-F101 §5.4"), but must **never reproduce standard text
  verbatim or attach licensed documents** — knowledge is synthesised in our own
  words. Everything published to the channel is open material.
- **In domain.** Off-domain asks get a friendly one-line redirect; IT/computer
  questions are not this channel.
